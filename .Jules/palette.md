## 2026-03-24 - Explaining Disabled Dual-State Actions\n**Learning:** Users often get confused when a high-value action button (like an AI Transition) is disabled without feedback. This is especially problematic for dual-state requirements (e.g., needing tracks on *both* Deck A and Deck B). Relying on users to guess the prerequisites from the interface layout creates friction.\n**Action:** Always provide explicit visual feedback (like `opacity-50 cursor-not-allowed`) and a descriptive `title` attribute (tooltip) explaining exactly *why* a complex button is disabled (e.g., 'Load tracks on both decks to transition') rather than just making it unresponsive.
